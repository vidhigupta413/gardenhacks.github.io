Beware of the Pests!!!

Japanese Beetles (Popillia japonica)

  • Food: Especially like to eat roses. Some of their other favourites
    include beans, grapes, and raspberries.
  • Geographic Location: Found mainly in the Easter and Miswestern
    Parts of the United States
  • History: Were initially introduced in 1900 by accident.
  • Other/Extra info: They attack in groups. This makes gardens
    especially vulnerable to Japanese Beetle invasions as they can
    affect a area
  • How do you know if you have been invaded?If you find...
    • Leaves with only their veins left behind
    • Dead patches of grass
  • Prevention Methods:
    • Plant choice - choose plants that japanese beetles don't
      prefer to feed on.
    • Strategic Planting - plant plants near garlic or rue to
      repel them.
  • Mediation Methods:
    • Row Covers
    • Handpic
    • Neem Oil
    • Set Japanese Beetle Traps

Cutworms

  • Food: Likes to eat green beans, cabbage, corn, and lettuce.
  • Geographic Location: Found all over the United States.
  • How do you know if you have been invaded?If you find...
    • The bases of plants chewed on
    • Seedlings stems that are damaged
    • Grass growth that has been damaged
  • Prevention Methods:
    • Plant choice - choose plants that japanese beetles don't
      prefer to feed on.
    • Strategic Planting - plant plants near garlic or rue to
      repel them.
  • Mediation Methods:
    • Hand pick - brave the cutworms and pick them away from you
      plants!
    • Make your garden bird friendly - to attract birds that can
      help pick the cutworms away.
    • Introduce predator insects - they will eat the cutworm
      population and keep them in check.

Squash Vine Borers

  • Food: Most commonly feed on squash, varieties of gourd, pumpkins,
    and zucchinis.
  • Geographic Location: Can be found in eastern parts of the U.S.
  • How do you know if you have been invaded?If you find...
    • Wilted leaves
    • Chewed on stems
    • Holes at the base of plants
  • Prevention Methods:
    • Plant as early as possible - this will allow plants to grow storng
      and allow them to fair better against the damage inflicted by the
      borer.
    • Avoiding replanting in the same area year after year - this will
      keep borers from last season from feeding on your seedlings.
    • Till the soil - to help eliminate pupae.
  • Mediation Methods:
    • Hand pick - pick away the borers attacking your plants! Slits the
      stems of plants to remove them.
    • Sprinkle diatomaceous earth
    • Sprinkle black pepper
    • Trap them - set up yellow sticky traps.

Tomato Hornworms (Manduca quinquemaculata)/Tobacco Hornworms (Manduca sexta)

  • Food: Enjoy feeding on tomatoes, tobacco, eggplants, peppers
    and potatoes.
  • Geographic Location: Found in the U.S. and Canada.
  • How do you know if you have been invaded?If you find...
    • Wilted, chewed on or missing leaves
    • Green/black droppings on leaves
    • Missing stems
  • Prevention Methods:
    • Till your soil - to eliminate larvae from the winter.
    • Interplanting - plant dill, basil, or marigolds to repel
      hornworms.
  • Mediation Methods:
    • Hand pick - pick away the hornworms.
    • Introduce predators like wasps - they can predate on the
      hornworm population and keep it in check.
